How it works

Write your question, add two to ten options, and press create. You get a short link that you can paste into a group chat, an email, a Slack channel, or a tweet. Everyone who opens it picks an answer and immediately sees how the votes are stacking up. There is nothing to download and no account to make, so the people you share it with can vote in a couple of seconds.

What it is good for

Quick team decisions like where to eat or which day works for a meeting, audience questions during a stream or webinar, settling a friendly argument, gathering feedback on a name or a logo, or running a fast pulse check with a community. It is built for speed and clarity rather than locked-down voting, so it shines for low-stakes questions where you just want an answer fast.

Private by default

Votes are anonymous. The poll stores only the question, the options, and a running tally of how many votes each option got. There are no names, no logins, and no tracking of who picked what. Each browser gets one vote per poll, which keeps casual polls honest without making people jump through hoops.

Do I need an account?

No. You create a poll and share the link in one step, with no sign-up and no email required.

How many options can I add?

Between two and ten. Add or remove rows until it looks right, then create the poll.

Can people vote more than once?

Each browser is allowed one vote per poll. It is designed for quick, friendly polls, not secured elections.

Is there a limit on votes or polls?

No. Make as many polls as you like and collect as many votes as you need, free.
